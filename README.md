# Healthcare Network Security: Literature Review

**Network Security Challenges in Sub-Saharan African Healthcare Infrastructure: A Systematic Review**

A structured narrative literature review examining the state of network
security in resource-constrained clinical environments, with focus on
Sub-Saharan Africa. Synthesises approximately 74 peer-reviewed papers
(2015–2025) and identifies four critical research gaps.

---

## Summary

Healthcare institutions across Sub-Saharan Africa are rapidly digitising,
deploying EHR systems and networked medical devices on infrastructure not
designed with security in mind. This review identifies:

- Why existing intrusion detection systems fail in low-resource clinical settings
- What ML approaches show promise for deployment in constrained environments
- Four specific research gaps that motivate the proposed PhD research

## Key findings

The review identifies four dimensions of the gap between state-of-the-art
ML-based anomaly detection and what is actually deployable in a district
hospital in Ghana:

1. **Data scarcity** — No labelled attack data exists on most clinical networks
2. **Edge deployment** — Standard models are too large for clinical hardware
3. **Concept drift** — Clinical traffic patterns change; models become stale
4. **Federated privacy** — Patient data in logs cannot be centralised for training

Each gap maps directly to a research question for the proposed PhD.
